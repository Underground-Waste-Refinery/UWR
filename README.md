# Underground Waste Refinery (UWR)

**A modular, serviceable, distributed system for thermal shock separation and catalytic pyrolysis of municipal solid waste.**

This repository contains the complete concept white paper for the **Underground Waste Refinery** — a decentralized waste processing unit designed to replace conventional underground waste containers. The system integrates proven industrial technologies (steam explosion, surfactant cleaning, near‑infrared sorting, electrostatic separation, and catalytic pyrolysis) into a factory‑built, swappable module that can be lowered into a prepared vault and automatically connected to utilities.

## 🔧 Key Features

- **Decentralized processing** – installed at neighborhood waste container sites, eliminating long‑haul trucking.
- **Integrated cleaning & sorting** – steam explosion separates organics; hot‑water washing, drying, NIR PVC rejection, and electrostatic sorting produce clean plastic flakes.
- **Catalytic pyrolysis** – converts recovered plastics into liquid fuel and syngas; syngas powers the process, and waste heat is recycled.
- **Modular, swappable design** – all equipment is housed in a steel module that can be lowered into a vault and automatically connected. Routine maintenance via surface hatches; entire module replaced for major overhauls.
- **Closed‑loop water treatment** – membrane bioreactor (MBR) and dissolved air flotation (DAF) recycle wash water, minimising discharge.
- **AI‑assisted adaptive control** – ultrasonic volume reduction monitoring and camera‑based solids quality feedback allow the system to self‑calibrate to varying waste composition.
- **Noise & vibration control** – staged decompression, anti‑vibration mounts, acoustic enclosures, and daytime scheduling ensure compliance with residential noise limits.

## 🧪 Project Status

**Concept phase – ready for prototyping.**  
The design is mature and has undergone several rounds of external technical review. The next steps are:

1. **3D CAD & docking design** – confirm spatial feasibility and interface details.
2. **Prototype construction** – build a test module and validate performance with real municipal solid waste.
3. **Pilot installation** – deploy at a controlled site for regulatory approval and operational data collection.
